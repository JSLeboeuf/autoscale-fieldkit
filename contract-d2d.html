WHEREAS, AutoScale AI Inc. develops and operates AI-powered voice receptionist and appointment booking software services for businesses (the "Service"); and WHEREAS, Representative desires to market and sell the Service as an independent contractor pursuant to the terms and conditions set forth herein; NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants herein, the parties agree as follows:

§ 1 Independent Contractor Status

1.1 No Employment Relationship. Representative is an independent contractor, not an employee, agent, partner, or joint venturer of the Company. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employment relationship between the parties.

1.2 No Benefits. Representative is not entitled to, and shall not receive, any employee benefits, including without limitation workers' compensation, unemployment insurance, health insurance, retirement plans, paid leave, or any other benefit that Company provides to its employees.

1.3 Tax Obligations. Representative is solely responsible for all federal, state, and local income taxes, self-employment taxes, and any other taxes or assessments arising from amounts paid under this Agreement. Company will issue Form 1099-NEC to Representative for any compensation exceeding $600 in a calendar year. Representative shall provide a completed Form W-9 prior to receiving any compensation.

1.4 Business Expenses. Representative is solely responsible for all costs and expenses incurred in the performance of services under this Agreement, including but not limited to transportation, meals, lodging, phone, internet, and equipment.

1.5 Control of Work. Representative retains the right to set their own hours, choose their own methods, and determine their own schedule for performing services, subject to the terms of this Agreement. Representative is free to provide services to other businesses, provided they do not conflict with the Non-Compete obligations set forth in Section 11.

§ 2 Scope of Services

2.1 Services. Representative agrees to solicit potential customers for the Company's AI receptionist and voice booking services through door-to-door and in-person sales activities within the Territory defined in Section 10.

2.2 Company-Provided Resources. Company shall provide Representative with the following resources at no cost:

  • Access to sales presentation materials, digital pitch decks, and product demos
  • Access to field sales tools at the designated representative portal URL
  • Read-only access to the Company CRM for leads assigned by Representative
  • Training materials and onboarding documentation
  • Unique representative tracking ID (TID) for attribution of sales

2.3 Representative Obligations. Representative shall: (a) present the Service accurately and in good faith; (b) comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws; (c) not make any representations or warranties beyond those authorized by Company; (d) promptly report customer complaints to Company; and (e) maintain professional conduct at all times while representing AutoScale AI.

§ 4 Commission Structure
20% Rep Commission of gross revenue (what the client pays) on setup + monthly
8% Area Manager Override on all sales made by reps under their supervision

4.1 Rep Commission. Representative shall earn a commission equal to twenty percent (20%) of the gross revenue collected from each client they close. Gross revenue means the total amount billed to the client (setup fee + monthly subscription), before any deductions. This commission applies to both the one-time setup fee and each monthly subscription payment.

4.2 Commission Duration. Rep commission on each client is paid for a maximum of twelve (12) months from the client's initial payment date. After 12 months, no further commission is owed on that client. Representative continues to earn commission on new clients they close.

4.3 Area Manager Override. If Representative serves as an Area Manager supervising other Representatives, they shall earn an additional override of eight percent (8%) of gross revenue collected from clients closed by Representatives under their supervision. The override follows the same 12-month duration per client. When an Area Manager closes a sale personally, they earn both the 20% rep commission and the 8% override (28% total).

4.4 Commission Attribution. Sales are attributed to Representative via the unique tracking ID (TID) embedded in the pay link. Representative is responsible for ensuring clients use their assigned TID link. Sales without a valid TID are not eligible for commission.

4.5 Payment Timing. Commissions are disbursed within 5 business days of commission approval. Company shall approve commissions once the client's payment has cleared and funds are available to Company. Recurring commissions are approved monthly, within 5 business days of the close of each billing cycle.

4.6 Chargebacks and Refunds. If a client successfully initiates a chargeback, payment dispute, or if Company issues a refund for any reason, the corresponding commission shall be deducted from Representative's next commission payment or, if no future payment is due, Representative shall reimburse Company within thirty (30) days of written notice.

Clawback — 90 Days: If a client cancels their subscription or fails to pay within the first ninety (90) days following their initial payment, all commissions paid on that client shall be deducted from Representative's future earnings. After 90 days, commissions on that client are fully earned and non-recoverable.

4.7 Commission Adjustment Schedule. Commission rates may be adjusted as the Company scales, per the following schedule tied to Company's Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR):

Company ARR Rep Commission Manager Override
$0 — $1M 20% 8%
$1M — $3M 15% 5%
$3M+ 10% 3%

Commission adjustments apply to new clients closed after the ARR threshold is reached. Existing client commissions remain at the rate in effect when the client was closed, for the remainder of their 12-month commission period.

4.8 Commission Statements. Company shall provide Representative with a written commission statement for each payment issued, itemizing the clients, amounts, and calculation basis for all commissions paid.

§ 5 Data Ownership & Intellectual Property

5.1 Company Ownership. All customer data, lead information, contact records, call recordings, AI conversation logs, voice transcripts, business intelligence, and any other data generated or collected in connection with the Service or sales process is and shall remain the exclusive property of AutoScale AI Inc. This includes data entered into Company systems by Representative.

5.2 No Personal Use of Data. Representative shall not copy, export, store, share, sell, or otherwise misappropriate any customer data or Company data for any purpose other than performing their obligations under this Agreement.

5.3 AI Training Data. Any call recordings, conversation scripts, objection-handling data, or other materials arising from Representative's sales activities may be used by Company to train and improve its AI systems. Representative grants Company a perpetual, royalty-free, worldwide license to use such materials for this purpose.

5.4 Survival. The data ownership provisions of this Section survive termination of this Agreement indefinitely.

5.5 Return of Materials. Upon termination of this Agreement, Representative shall promptly return or destroy all Company materials, customer data, and proprietary information in their possession and certify such destruction or return in writing upon request.

§ 6 Confidentiality & Non-Disclosure

6.1 Confidential Information. "Confidential Information" means any non-public information of Company, including without limitation: pricing structures, commission rates, technology stack, software architecture, system prompts, AI model configurations, customer lists, prospect databases, business plans, marketing strategies, financial data, and the terms of this Agreement.

6.2 Obligations. Representative shall: (a) hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence; (b) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent; (c) use Confidential Information solely to perform their obligations under this Agreement; and (d) protect Confidential Information with the same degree of care used to protect their own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

6.3 Exclusions. Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of Representative; (b) was rightfully known to Representative prior to disclosure; or (c) is required to be disclosed by law or court order, provided Representative gives Company prompt written notice before disclosure.

6.4 Duration. Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement for a period of two (2) years from the date of termination.

§ 7 Non-Compete & Non-Solicitation

7.1 Non-Compete. During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twenty-four (24) months following termination, Representative shall not, directly or indirectly, market, sell, promote, represent, or solicit sales for any product or service that is substantially similar to the Company's AI voice receptionist or AI appointment booking services. This restriction is limited to the AI voice AI and AI receptionist category and does not prohibit Representative from engaging in other software sales or business activities outside this category.

7.2 Non-Solicitation of Customers. During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twenty-four (24) months following termination, Representative shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, contact, or induce any customer or prospective customer introduced to Company by Representative (or otherwise known to Representative through their engagement with Company) to reduce, cancel, or transfer their business away from Company.

7.3 Non-Solicitation of Personnel. During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twenty-four (24) months following termination, Representative shall not, directly or indirectly, recruit, solicit, or induce any other Company representative or employee to terminate their relationship with Company.

7.4 Geographic Scope. The non-compete restriction applies within the United States. The non-solicitation restrictions apply globally and are not geographically limited.

§ 8 Term & Termination

8.1 Term. This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until terminated by either party pursuant to the provisions of this Section.

8.2 Termination Without Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement without cause upon ninety (90) days' prior written notice to the other party.

8.3 Termination for Cause. Company may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if Representative: (a) commits fraud or makes material misrepresentations to customers; (b) violates any applicable law or regulation; (c) breaches Section 5 (Data Ownership), Section 6 (Confidentiality), Section 7 (Non-Compete), or engages in misconduct that could harm Company's reputation; or (d) fails to cure a material breach within ten (10) days of written notice. Representative may terminate for cause if Company materially breaches its payment obligations and fails to cure within fifteen (15) days of written notice.

8.4 Post-Termination Commissions. Following termination for any reason other than for cause by Company, Representative shall continue to receive commissions on existing active clients for the remainder of each client's 12-month commission period (Section 4.2), but no longer than six (6) months following the termination effective date. No commissions shall be owed for new clients acquired after the termination date. Termination for cause by Company results in immediate forfeiture of all unpaid and future commissions.

8.5 Effect of Termination. Upon termination: (a) Representative shall immediately cease representing themselves as affiliated with Company; (b) all rights and licenses granted herein terminate immediately; (c) Representative shall return all Company materials; and (d) the following provisions survive: Sections 5, 6, 7, 9, 12, and 14.

§ 9 Liability, Insurance & Indemnification

9.1 Company Responsibilities. Company is responsible for the Service, its functionality, AI performance, uptime, data security, customer technical support, and billing. Company represents that it maintains Errors & Omissions (E&O) insurance appropriate for its business activities.

9.2 Representative Responsibilities. Representative is solely responsible for their own conduct, statements, and actions during sales activities. Representative shall not make any guarantee, representation, or warranty regarding the Service beyond those set forth in Company-approved materials. Representative is responsible for compliance with all federal, state, and local sales, solicitation, and consumer protection laws.

9.3 Insurance. Representative is strongly encouraged to obtain and maintain general liability insurance with a minimum coverage of $1,000,000 per occurrence. Representative shall name Company as an additional insured upon written request.

9.4 Indemnification by Representative. Representative shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Company, its officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to: (a) Representative's breach of this Agreement; (b) Representative's negligence or willful misconduct; (c) any misrepresentation made by Representative to a customer; or (d) Representative's violation of applicable law.

9.5 Indemnification by Company. Company shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Representative from and against any claims arising out of or relating to: (a) the Service itself (excluding misuse or misrepresentation by Representative); or (b) Company's breach of this Agreement.

9.6 Limitation of Liability. IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, PUNITIVE, OR CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOST PROFITS, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES. COMPANY'S AGGREGATE LIABILITY TO REPRESENTATIVE SHALL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL COMMISSIONS PAID TO REPRESENTATIVE IN THE THREE (3) MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

§ 10 Territory

10.1 Assigned Territory. Company assigns Representative the following United States market(s):   (the "Territory"). Territory is non-exclusive unless Performance Exclusivity is earned per Section 10.3.

10.2 No Default Exclusive Rights. This Agreement does not grant Representative any exclusive rights to any territory by default. Company reserves the right to appoint additional representatives in the same Territory, sell directly to customers in the Territory, and to expand or modify territory boundaries at any time.

10.3 Performance Exclusivity. Representative may earn exclusive rights to their Territory by maintaining a minimum of ten (10) active paying clients per calendar quarter within the Territory. If Representative falls below this threshold for two consecutive quarters, exclusivity reverts to non-exclusive status automatically.

10.4 Territory Expansion. Additional territories may be added by mutual written agreement of the parties.

§ 11 Representations & Warranties

11.1 Mutual Representations. Each party represents and warrants that: (a) they have full authority to enter into this Agreement; (b) the Agreement does not conflict with any other obligation they have; and (c) they will comply with all applicable laws in performing their obligations.

11.2 Representative Representations. Representative additionally represents and warrants that: (a) they are not subject to any non-compete, non-solicitation, or other restrictive agreement that would prohibit performance under this Agreement; (b) they have no criminal convictions involving fraud, theft, or dishonesty; and (c) they will accurately represent the Service in all customer interactions.

§ 12 Dispute Resolution & Governing Law

12.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Province of Quebec, Canada, without regard to its conflict of law provisions.

12.2 Arbitration. Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement, or the breach thereof, shall be settled by binding arbitration administered by the ADR Institute of Canada under its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall take place in Quebec City, Quebec, or, at the mutual agreement of the parties, via videoconference. The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding, and judgment may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

12.3 Class Action Waiver. Representative waives any right to participate in a class action lawsuit or class-wide arbitration against Company.

12.4 Injunctive Relief. Notwithstanding Section 12.2, either party may seek injunctive or other equitable relief in any court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm, particularly with respect to breaches of Sections 5, 6, and 7 of this Agreement.

§ 13 General Provisions

13.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous negotiations, representations, warranties, and agreements, whether oral or written.

13.2 Amendments. This Agreement may not be amended, modified, or supplemented except by a written instrument signed by authorized representatives of both parties.

13.3 Waiver. No waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing. Failure to enforce any right or provision shall not constitute a waiver of such right or provision.

13.4 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect. If a court finds a non-compete or non-solicitation provision overly broad, such provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable ("blue pencil" doctrine).

13.5 Assignment. Representative may not assign or transfer any rights or obligations under this Agreement without prior written consent of Company. Company may assign this Agreement in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all of its assets.

13.6 Notices. All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered by: (a) hand delivery; (b) overnight courier; (c) certified mail, return receipt requested; or (d) email with confirmed receipt. Notices to Company shall be addressed to its registered agent or principal place of business. Notices to Representative shall be at the address set forth on the signature page.

13.7 Force Majeure. Neither party shall be liable for delays or failures in performance resulting from circumstances beyond their reasonable control, including acts of God, government actions, labor disputes, or infrastructure failures.

13.8 Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, including by electronic signature (e.g., DocuSign, Adobe Sign), each of which shall be deemed an original and together constitute one and the same instrument.
